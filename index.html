About Us

History

Compostela National High School traces its roots to 1966 as Compostela High School, initially operating at Compostela Central Elementary School. In 1969, it acquired its current 5-hectare site and was nationalized in 1972, becoming Compostela National High School. The school has grown significantly, adding new buildings and facilities over the decades, including a two-story building in 1999 and another in 2008.

DepEd Mission

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment. We aim to develop teachers and learners who are globally competitive and committed to building a just and humane society.

DepEd Vision

We dream of Filipinos who passionately love their country and whose values and competencies enable them to realize their full potential and contribute meaningfully to building the nation. As a learner-centered public institution, the Department of Education continuously improves itself to better serve its stakeholders.
